Mission

10x-ing the working class and lower middle class's share of GDP, sustainably.

Economists use GDP (the amount of money a country's population spends annually) to measure how developed a population is.

I don't think GDP is a great metric for measuring personal or societal growth, but then I grew up not getting to spend much time with my dad daily throughout my childhood because he was always working overtime to make ends meet; so I guess 10x-ing the amount of money the working class and the lower middle class can spend (through a Universal Basic Education, as I call it) might be a good entry point to eventually building the Universal Pretty Damn Good Education that helps people fulfill their potentials, regardless of their wealth, location or demographics.
‍
To appreciate the value of these, it's important to remember we live in a world in which most people live a lifetime and die without knowing what it is they can be great at. It breaks my heart.
‍
We live in a world that has hardly innovated in education, teaching or learning, with both the traditional education and edtech industries optimising for cash rather than pursuing intelligent growth that can sustain and scale.
That's Learning Loop's starting mission.
